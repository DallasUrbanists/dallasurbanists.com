What is This Dallas Life?

This Dallas Life is a blog about urbanism in Dallas. The blog focuses on walkability, housing, public transit, mobility, and sustainable city planning in the City of Dallas and the Greater Dallas area. Our purpose is to promote thoughtful and informed discussions about urban issues in the region.

The blog was created by Hexel Colorado in 2022. In addition to written articles, the blog also includes short-form videos on TikTok and the By Way of Dallas podcast.

Hexel Colorado

Hexel is a Dallas-native and lifelong DFW resident. He started This Dallas Life in December 2022 and is its primary contributor. Though involved in various form of community activism since he was a teenager, his interest in urbanism began in December 2021, when he sold his car to live car-free in Old East Dallas.

Hexel was appointed to DART’s Citizens Advisory Committee (CAC) in May 2022. The role of the members of this public advisory group is to serve "as a two-way communication link between the DART Board, the general public and various interest groups."

Connor Hulla

Connor is a contributor for This Dallas Life and moderator of the r/DART subreddit. In January 2022, Connor started the r/DART subreddit. After a year of moderating and nurturing the community with frequent posts and updates, the subreddit has grown to 609 followers, including multiple DART employees.

Connor is the team’s de facto human encyclopedia, having watched hours of DART board and committee meetings. Connor co-hosted the first episode of the By Way of Dallas podcast with Hexel. He also contributes to the blog.

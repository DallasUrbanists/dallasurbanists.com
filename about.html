What is This?

This Dallas Life is a publication focused on urbanist topics such as walkability, housing, public transit, mobility, and sustainable city planning in the City of Dallas and the Greater Dallas area. Our purpose is to promote thoughtful and informed discussions about urban issues in the region.

The publication produces a variety of original content, including a blog, short-form videos on TikTok, and the "By Way of Dallas" podcast.

Who is This?

Three transit nerds run This Dallas Life on the side, outside of their full-time jobs. Coincidentally, they all happen to have day jobs as software engineers.

Where is This?

While our content occasionally explores other cities and suburbs in the Dallas - Fort Worth Metroplex, This Dallas Life primarily focuses on the City of Dallas and the other twelve DART member cities: Richardson, Garland, Plano, Addison, Carrollton, Cockrell Hill, Farmers Branch, Glenn Heights, Highland Park, Irving, Plano, Rowlett, and University Park.

Why This?

Throughout 2022, Hexel had been writing about Dallas urbanism on his personal blog, Instagram, and Twitter. Having accumulated a considerable amount of writing and original content about Dallas, it didn’t help that his last name was “Colorado.” Thus, he acquired the domain name ThisDallasLife.com and transitioned to underwriting under the moniker This Dallas Life.

This publication, This Dallas Life, was created in pursuit of a dream: of a more walkable, sustainable, and affordable Dallas; of our city breaking free from car-dependency; of the restoration of places destroyed by urban highways; of DFW cured of sprawl and following the path blazed anew by way of Dallas.

Does the podcast have anything to do with the clothing brand?

No. It’s just a coincidence that the name of our podcast about transportation and mobility, By Way of Dallas, has the same name of a locally renown clothing brand.

Why did we name our podcast that?

The phrase “by way of” is used to mean “By the route of; through; via.” One of the most common uses of this phrase is to briefly describe flight plans. For example, if someone asked me about my flight to Dallas and my itinerary includes a layover in Chicago, I may tell someone “I’m flying to Dallas by way of Chicago.” Considering DFW is the second busiest airport in the world, it’s likely millions of people have uttered the phrase “by way of Dallas” to describe their brief stop on route to their final destination. This is how our podcast name directly relates to the topic of transportation.

In a philosophical sense, the podcast’s purpose is to present how the way people move in Dallas affects the way they live. Can you now see the connection between By Way of Dallas and This Dallas Life? In a fun sense, there’s potential for a double entendre. If someone listens to the podcast and learns something from hearing a story about someone in Dallas, they could say they “learned it by way of Dallas.” This turn of phrase would equally mean they learned something from the podcast called By Way of Dallas, as well as they learned something by way of the city itself.
